Walk Dr. Pisu through the three plots in order: population view (09a) → statistics (09b) → single run (09c). The obs_recovery time-series shows a large NLL elevation at approximately 25% through the normalized run (the avoidance maneuver), while obs_noescape stays flat throughout. The spike is not predictive (it happens during the maneuver, not before) but it IS diagnostic: if you see a spike, an avoidance maneuver is occurring. This validates that the digital twin's residual is sensitive to the right events.

The finding that CVaR over the full run is not significant is expected and actually informative: it tells us that the signal is concentrated in time (brief spike), not sustained. This means the correct operational use is rolling/windowed CVaR over a short window (e.g., last 50 windows = 5 seconds), not over the entire run. The framework does not need to wait for a full run to fire — it can trigger on a local CVaR spike.

The has_adjacent_lane result should be explained carefully: it appears in the wrong direction but the explanation is structural (noescape stops early, stays in multi-lane section) not a failure of the signal. The signal will be discriminative when we have actual single-lane vs. multi-lane conditions. Present this as "we know it will work but cannot validate it yet without the singlelane data."

The p=0.034 result with n=18 runs per group is modest but meaningful. Acknowledge this. The new data collection (24 additional runs across two conditions) will also increase statistical power when all four conditions are combined in an ablation study.
