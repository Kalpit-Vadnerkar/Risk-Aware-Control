INTERPRETATION: WHAT THE RESULTS TELL US

The identifiability problem (fundamental, not a model limitation):
  obs_noescape: vehicle decelerates toward obstacle → trajectory locally identical to deceleration at traffic light
  obs_recovery: vehicle performs lateral avoidance → trajectory is anomalous relative to training distribution
  → No trajectory-based signal can distinguish "stopping because blocked" from "stopping because planned"
  → This is not fixed by a better model — it is a structural property of the observation space

What the ST-GAT residual CAN do:
  ✓ Detect anomalous maneuvers (lane changes, emergency avoidance) — validated p=0.034
  ✓ Distinguish "vehicle doing something unexpected" from "vehicle on nominal trajectory"
  ✗ Predict BEFORE an obstacle encounter whether avoidance will be needed
  ✗ Distinguish obstacle approach from traffic-light deceleration during the approach phase

What this means for the framework:
  → Signal 3 (residual) is a CONFIRMATION signal, not a PREDICTION signal
  → It fires DURING avoidance (reactive) to confirm a non-nominal event is occurring
  → Signals 1 (topology) and 2 (kinematics) are the PROACTIVE signals — they fire
    before the vehicle commits to any action, based on fixed environmental properties
  → The three signals are complementary: topology and kinematics are proactive but dumb;
    residual is smart but reactive — together they cover the decision space

Revised framework timing:
  → At t=0 (obstacle first detected): check topology (Signal 1) + compute TTC (Signal 2)
  → If both pass: enable avoidance contingent on Signal 3 confirming behavioral anomaly
  → If Signal 3 does not fire within τ windows: revert to MRC (obstacle may be spurious)
  → This creates a "hold and confirm" logic: geometry + kinematics say "feasible",
    digital twin confirms "actually happening"
