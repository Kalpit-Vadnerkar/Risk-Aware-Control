THE THREE-SIGNAL FEASIBILITY CLASSIFIER

Decision logic — all three conditions must hold for avoidance to be enabled:

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  Signal 1 (Topology):   has_adjacent_lane(ego_pos, HD_map)?    │
  │  Signal 2 (Kinematic):  TTC = obj_dist / v_approach > τ_min?  │
  │  Signal 3 (Behavioral): CVaR_α(NLL residual_t) > θ ?          │
  │                                                                  │
  │  IF all three → AVOIDANCE WARRANTED → override policy=auto     │
  │  IF any fails → MRC MANDATORY      → maintain policy=manual    │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

What each signal handles:

  Signal 1 (Topology):
    → Fails when: obstacle on single-lane road, no driveable neighbor
    → Deterministic: HD map lookup, no uncertainty
    → Cannot distinguish: multi-lane with adequate time vs. multi-lane too close

  Signal 2 (Kinematic):
    → Fails when: obstacle detected too late (<5m), no time for path planning
    → Semi-deterministic: depends on perception accuracy of distance/speed
    → Cannot distinguish: adjacent lane exists vs. does not exist

  Signal 3 (Behavioral — digital twin):
    → Fails when: situation appears nominally plausible (normal deceleration)
    → Probabilistic: CVaR from learned distribution residual
    → Necessary because: Signals 1+2 alone cannot distinguish real obstacles
      from routine stops (traffic lights, crosswalks) — no trajectory anomaly exists
      until the vehicle has already committed to a stop

Integration point: signals are evaluated at 10 Hz; threshold τ_min and θ calibrated
on held-out nominal runs using conformal prediction (finite-sample coverage guarantee)
