The AND-logic structure is important and should be stressed: this is NOT a learned end-to-end classifier. It is a structured decision gate where each component is independently interpretable and has a clear failure mode it addresses. This is intentional — it preserves the interpretability that ISO 26262 functional safety requires, and it allows each component to be independently validated and potentially replaced.

The question "why do you need Signal 3 at all if you have Signals 1 and 2?" is the critical one. The answer: an obstacle at 30m on a multi-lane road at 7 m/s satisfies Signals 1 and 2 — but so does an AV decelerating at a red light at 30m on a multi-lane road at 7 m/s. Signal 3 (the digital twin residual) is what distinguishes a real unexpected obstacle from normal planned deceleration. Without it, the framework would fire avoidance at every traffic light. This is the novel contribution of the digital twin component.

Dr. Pisu will likely ask: "What is the latency of Signal 3?" Answer: the residual is computed per 3-second window at 10Hz (stride=1), so it is effectively available at 10Hz with 3 seconds of history. The total system latency from detection to override signal is under 100ms, which is within Autoware's planning cycle time.
