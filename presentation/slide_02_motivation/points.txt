THE PROBLEM: AV Safety Defaults Are Binary and Static

Scenario: autonomous vehicle encounters an unexpected static obstacle at 30m, traveling at 7 m/s

Current behavior (Autoware default):
  → Minimum Risk Condition (MRC): decelerate to a complete stop in-lane
  → Conservative, always "safe" in the collision-avoidance sense

But MRC creates secondary risks:
  → Stopped vehicle in active traffic lane → rear-end collision hazard
  → Traffic blockage, mission failure, emergency vehicle obstruction
  → In urban environments, MRC may be the MORE dangerous outcome

The right action depends on context:
  → Adjacent lane available + enough lead time → avoidance is safer
  → No adjacent lane OR obstacle too close → MRC is correct
  → Current systems cannot distinguish these cases at runtime

The gap: avoidance policy is a fixed parameter (manual vs. auto), not a runtime decision
  → No system assesses whether the specific situation makes avoidance warranted
