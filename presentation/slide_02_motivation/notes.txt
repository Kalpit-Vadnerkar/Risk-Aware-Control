The concrete motivating observation: in our Autoware + AWSIM experiments, we collected 18 runs where the vehicle encountered an obstacle at 30m on a two-lane road with a clear adjacent lane. With avoidance policy=manual (the Autoware default), the vehicle stops every time. With policy=auto, the vehicle successfully avoids in every run. The system has the geometric and kinematic capacity to avoid — it simply doesn't because the policy is fixed. The question we are addressing: can the digital twin give us a runtime signal to switch that policy dynamically, and can we prove it makes the right call?

The secondary hazard point (stopped vehicle in active lane) is well-documented in ISO 26262 SOTIF annexes and in traffic engineering literature. Emphasize that MRC is not always the "safe" choice — it trades collision risk against traffic risk. This framing connects directly to Dr. Pisu's risk assessment background.
