LITERATURE GAP: The Avoidance Decision Has No Owner

Body 1 — Avoidance PLANNING (well-studied):
  → MPC, CBF, polynomial trajectory optimization, reachability analysis
  → Assumption: the decision to avoid is already made upstream
  → Representative work: Ziegler et al. (2014), Brito et al. (2019), Pek & Althoff (2020)

Body 2 — Risk ASSESSMENT (well-studied):
  → Time-to-Collision (TTC), Post-Encroachment Time (PET), probabilistic risk bounds
  → Quantifies risk of current state but prescribes no action
  → Representative work: Jansson (2005), Lefèvre et al. (2014), our prior T-ITS 2025

Body 3 — Emergency Braking / AEB (well-studied):
  → Purely kinematic threshold logic: TTC < τ → brake
  → No lane availability check, no learned model, no distinction between obstacle types
  → ISO 22737, Euro NCAP AEB protocols

The gap — Avoidance DECISION-MAKING (unstudied):
  → No framework asks: "Given this specific situation, is avoidance the correct response?"
  → Systems default to MRC (stop) or avoidance (go) based on a static configuration parameter
  → The decision is made by the system integrator at deployment time, not by the vehicle at runtime

Our problem statement fills this gap:
  → Real-time binary classification: {avoidance warranted, MRC mandatory}
  → Validated across conditions where the correct answer differs
